Vegetable Soup (Edikaikong)

Description

Edikaikong is a popular Nigerian vegetable soup made with fresh spinach, waterleaf, assorted meats, and spices. It is rich in nutrients and often enjoyed with fufu, pounded yam, or eba.

This recipe guides you through the steps to prepare a flavorful pot of Edikaikong soup that serves 4 people.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of waterleaf, finely chopped
  • 3 cups of fresh spinach or ugwu leaves, chopped
  • 500g assorted meats (beef, tripe, and stockfish)
  • 200g smoked fish
  • 1 cup of periwinkles (optional)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of crayfish, ground
  • 1 cup of palm oil
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon of ground pepper

Steps for Cooking:

  1. Prepare the ingredients:
    • Clean and chop the waterleaf and spinach leaves, and set aside.
    • Boil the assorted meats with chopped onions, seasoning cubes, and salt until tender. Set aside.
  2. Cook the soup base:
    • In a large pot, heat the palm oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until translucent.
    • Add the ground crayfish, ground pepper, and scotch bonnet peppers. Stir and cook for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Add meats and vegetables:
    • Add the cooked meats, smoked fish, and periwinkles. Stir well to combine.
    • Gently fold in the waterleaf and spinach or Ugwu leaves. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Season and simmer:
    • Season with additional salt and seasoning cubes to taste. Cover and simmer for another 3-5 minutes.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve hot with your choice of fufu, pounded yam, or rice.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Edikaikong Soup!
